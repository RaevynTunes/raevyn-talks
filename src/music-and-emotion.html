I've listened to a lot of music over the years. My parents had the radio on every time we were in the car, and I realized something after a while:


I don't like the music on the radio.


This wasn't always the case, of course- there are always exceptions- but for the most part, I'd dislike what the stations were playing. My dad listened to hip-hop and rap, and my mom listened to R&B, and I had my issues with both.


With R&B, it was more of a taste thing. The music isn't exactly the most energetic and exciting, and I got bored of it pretty easily. Hip-hop and rap, however, was another story. I didn't really like what the songs were talking about- sex, drugs, and violence- and I decided to start listening to something else.
That 'something else' turned out to be electronic music.


Another thing that electronic gave me, other than a clearer head, was emotion. I like feeling things from songs, emotions and stories brought to my attention by music. Instead of just talking about myself, though, I'll start off by giving some examples of artists and songs that deliver.

modus

Paul, a.k.a modus, is an amazing artist and a great guy in general. His production is top tier for sure, and he's put out a lot of great tunes. You should check him and his stuff out (start with the No Limits LP and work up from there). The specific song I want to talk about, however, is Timeless: Over Me II with Sky Roses.

For one thing: this song is bangin'. Like, an absolute chune. How did he pull it off? I don't even know. He does have a breakdown on his Youtube channel, so you could watch that if you want some in-depth info.


Important factor number two: It tells a story; a love story, in fact. Usually I'm not a fan, but Paul and Sky pull it off somehow.

It's a story of an impossible love, hence the last line in the lyrics: 'I may not be able to love you, but I still want you to stay.' Our love is impossible, but I still appreciate you being around.

- modus, on the song's meaning

Skybreak

Skybreak is cracked. I'm not even joking, dude's a legend. Fusing melodicity and emotion into his tunes with grace and ease.

So, I was going to showcase the song below as an example, right, but then a new tune came out the day I was writing this, and it blew this one out of the water.

The song I'm actually showcasing is called Cryolite.

I was actually speechless when the song ended. The nostalgic vibe, the vocals (god, the vocals), and the overall atmosphere from that song, and the whole EP honestly, is what pushes this one over the top. A excellent tune and an amazing EP.

Pixel Terror

Pixel Terror's story is intresting. Originally starting out as a duo, they recently split (on a positive note, luckily), and Bentley took over the main name, while Ahrya became Afinity. I actually have two songs from when they were a duo to show off: Bittersweet and Collapse.

I picked these two because they kind of contrast. Bittersweet has a tense intro (that lead, sheesh) and goes into a heavy drop. An absolute classic. Collapse, on the other hand, is melodic throughout. It's still just as good, and there are some pretty nice vocals, courtesy of neverwaves. Two solid tunes.

What It's For

I feel like these songs illustrate emotion in the best way I could show you. However, you might be asking what this is all for. Why did I show you these? It's simple- I'm a producer, and I want to capture emotion like these songs do. I want the emotion to flow out of my music in the way these songs do. I want people to connect, and to feel something when they here my latest tunes. I think these artists can do it, and I want to as well.

Maybe you're an aspiring producer as well- who knows?- but I want to make it clear that emotion is an amazing thing to put into song form. Not every tune has to just be a heavy headbanging one.

That's all from me, though. If you didn't skip the rest of the song, then thanks for reading. If you did skip, that's okay too, we all have different attention spans. Have a nice day. - Lost Love
